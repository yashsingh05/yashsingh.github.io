Story

Mumbai → Syracuse,
7,800 miles for the data.

I started in Mumbai — an electronics & telecom engineering degree by day, and my first analyst roles in fintech insurance and real estate, where I learned that a good dashboard can end an argument faster than any meeting.

Analyzing 20,000 insurance claims with Spark taught me scale. Building ETL pipelines for fragmented property data taught me that most of data work is cleaning up the mess before the insight. I also published research on machine learning for early Alzheimer's detection — the healthcare side of my curiosity.

Then I moved to Syracuse, NY for my MS in Information Systems — and America's snowiest city handed me my favorite dataset. If it snows four feet, I want to know whose street gets plowed first. So I built a pipeline that finds out.
